Corporate Learning Camp 2026: Insights for Modern L&D | Alphabees

The Corporate Learning Camp 2026 delivered valuable insights on self-directed learning, digital sovereignty, and AI-powered professional development – topics that L&D leaders should have on their radar now.

The Corporate Learning Camp 2026 (#CLC26) took place in mid-March in Hamburg and once again demonstrated where the professional development landscape in the DACH region is heading. Over 250 participants – L&D professionals from companies, universities, academies, and training providers – discussed the most pressing questions about modern learning culture both on-site and online. This year's motto "learning beyond teaching" strikes a chord: How can we successfully transition from traditional instruction to self-directed, needs-based learning?

For decision-makers in education, CLC26 offers valuable insights. Anyone responsible for professional development programs should be familiar with the key topics – and assess which ones are relevant to their own institution.

Self-Directed Learning as the New Guiding Principle

The motto "learning beyond teaching" describes a fundamental shift in perspective. The focus is no longer primarily on delivering knowledge, but on supporting learners in learning independently and situationally. This development has far-reaching implications for designing professional development programs.

For universities, academies, and corporate training, this means: learning environments must be designed to enable individual learning – independent of fixed course schedules and without constant supervision by instructors. This is precisely where modern AI tutors come in. They serve as always-available learning companions that answer questions, explain concepts, and guide learners through complex content.

The Alphabees AI Tutor for Moodle embodies this approach: It integrates directly into existing courses and is available to learners around the clock. This transforms "learning beyond teaching" from a buzzword into lived practice – without requiring education providers to overhaul their entire infrastructure.

Digital Sovereignty Gains Importance

A recurring theme at CLC26 was digital sovereignty. The community is increasingly embracing decentralized platforms like Mastodon and the Fediverse. Behind this lies the desire to communicate independently of major US platforms and retain control over data.

For L&D leaders, this topic is relevant for several reasons. First, data protection requirements – particularly GDPR – pose significant barriers to using many commercial tools. Second, there is growing awareness that dependence on individual providers carries strategic risks. Third, learners increasingly expect their data to be processed securely and transparently.

The choice of learning platform plays a central role here. Moodle as an open-source system offers clear advantages: institutions retain control over their data and can customize the platform to their own requirements. An AI tutor that integrates seamlessly with Moodle fits into this strategy and expands capabilities without creating new dependencies.

AI in Professional Development: From Hype to Practice

Artificial intelligence was also a prominent topic at CLC26 – albeit with a notable shift. While previous years often focused on fundamental questions, this time the emphasis was on practical applications. How can AI be meaningfully integrated into learning processes? Which tasks can it take over, and which not?

For HR developers and L&D professionals, this opens up concrete areas for action. AI-powered tutors can handle repetitive tasks: they answer frequently asked questions, provide feedback on exercises, and help learners navigate extensive course materials. This relieves instructors and allows them to focus on more complex support tasks.

At the same time, AI tutors scale without additional staffing requirements. Whether ten or a thousand learners – support remains available. For institutions that need to manage growing participant numbers, this is a decisive advantage.

Community Learning and Knowledge Management

Another focus of CLC26 was knowledge management. Discussions centered on how personal and organizational knowledge can be better structured and shared. An announced community book project on "Personal Knowledge Management" shows that there is demand in this area.

For education providers, this contains an important insight: learning doesn't end with course completion. Sustainable professional development requires structures that keep knowledge accessible and support continuous learning. An AI tutor can serve as an intelligent interface here – it knows the course content and helps learners refresh or deepen their knowledge even after completion.

The regional Corporate Learning Communities, which were also represented at the camp, further demonstrate: networking and exchange are key success factors for effective professional development. Digital tools can support this networking, but they cannot replace personal interaction.

Conclusion: Translating Insights into Practice

The Corporate Learning Camp 2026 demonstrated that the professional development landscape is undergoing profound change. Self-directed learning, digital sovereignty, and the targeted use of AI are no longer future topics – they already shape the daily work of L&D professionals today.

For decision-makers at universities, academies, and in corporate training, the question is how to translate these insights into their own practice.
